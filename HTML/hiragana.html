This is, or will be, a full list of the Hiragana characters in Japanese. Hiragana characters are used in natural sentences for native Japanese words, particles, or words without kanji. They are phonetic only, much like English letters. The first five vowels are the only vowels in the alphabet, and they are only pronounced as directed, with no exeptions. That makes the language a little easier to master. After the first five vowels, most of the characters are pronounced as a consonant followed by a vowel, save for a few exeptions to be covered when the time comes. I reccomend studying this page as well as the Katakana page, for words on all other flashcards will be written in one of these languages. As for telling the two languages apart, keep in mind that you will have memorized them both. Hiragana is more akin to English's cursive, and Katakana is much sharper in shape. Enjoy!
