This page will contain the full list of Katakana, the second alphabet of Japanese. This alphabet has the same sounds as Hiragana, simply with a different purpose. Katakana is used to write words that are not Japanese, such as sandwhich or pen. You would not write pen in Hiragana, only in Katakana. This alphabet is also used for onomatopoeia and for the Chinese readings of Kanji, onyomi. Onyomi is the catagory of kanji pronounciation when the characters are strung together to create one word. They are usually only a syllable or two, because they are meant to be only one note and connotation within a word. This is written in Katakana, because it was originally from China. This alphabet can be picked out for its sharp angles and bold strokes. Enjoy!
