Adjectives in Japanese come in two forms: i- and na-. We will briefly cover na-adjectives on this page. Na-adjectives are adjectives that end in na when in front of a noun, and are treated as nouns most of the time. For example, in the sentence まちが しずか です (the town is quiet), しずか is a na-adjective meaning "quiet". However, as you might notice, the word does not end in na. That is because na is only used when the adjective is directly in front of and describing a noun, as seen in しずかな まちです, (the quiet town). They are also conjugated as nouns. Enjoy!

しずか（な）

quiet

きれい（な）

clean, pretty

べんり（な）

convenient

たいせつ（な）

important, precious

じょうず（な）

skillful; good at

へた（な）

unskilled; bad at
